研究論文

『魏志倭人伝』「邪馬壹國」表記の音韻学的分析

科学が拓く邪馬台国論争の新地平

100年以上にわたる邪馬台国論争の膠着を打ち破る、革命的な音韻学的論証。本稿は、最古級の写本が示す原初の記録「邪馬壹國」を「3世紀音韻変化期モデル」で科学的に分析。その結果、「壹」が九州「山門」の「詰まるト（甲類）」の音の輪郭を捉えた唯一の合理的音写であり、後代の「臺」表記が音韻的に成立しないことを証明する。文献学、軍事史、認知科学を統合し、論争の前提そのものを覆す。

初代天皇は化学者だった

日本書紀『無水造飴』の暗号、1300年の謎を解く

日本の建国神話に隠された、驚くべき科学技術の真実。初代天皇・神武が行ったとされる奇妙な儀式「無水造飴」は、神話ではなく、辰砂から戦略物資である水銀を精製する高度な化学プロセスを記述した「暗号化された技術書」であった。鉛同位体比分析や考古科学の物証を駆使し、ヤマト王権が武力ではなく技術覇権によって国家を築いたという、日本の起源を覆す新たな歴史像を提示する。

Research Papers

Phonological Analysis of the "Yama-ichi-koku" Notation in the *Wei Zhi*

The Final Conclusion to the Yamatai Debate, Unraveled by Scientific Phonology

This paper aims to solve Japan's most enduring historical mystery: the location of the lost kingdom of Yamatai. It presents a revolutionary phonological argument that breaks the century-long stalemate, scientifically analyzing the original "Yama-ichi-koku" notation from the oldest extant manuscripts. Using the "3rd Century Phonological Transition Model," it proves that "ichi" (壹) was the only rational phonetic transcription for the unique sound of "Yamato" in Kyushu, and that the later "tai" (臺) notation is phonetically impossible.

Japan's First Emperor Was a Chemist

Decoding the 1300-Year-Old Cipher of "Syrup-Making Without Water" in the *Nihon Shoki*

Discover the astonishing scientific truth hidden in Japan's foundation myth. The peculiar ritual of "syrup-making without water" performed by the first emperor, Jinmu, was not a myth but a coded technical manual describing the advanced chemical process of distilling mercury from cinnabar. Using evidence from lead isotope analysis and archaeoscience, this paper presents a new history of Japan's origin, where the Yamato dynasty established its rule not by force, but by technological supremacy.

Ancient Chinese Audio Player

A Digital Tool to Reconstruct the "Sounds" of the 3rd Century

A web tool developed during the research for the "Yama-ichi-koku" phonological analysis. It allows users to play and compare reconstructed Old Chinese sounds of Kanji based on major reconstruction systems (Starostin, Baxter, Zhengzhang, etc.). This tool is made available to help simulate the sounds actually heard by ancient scribes and to explore the phonetic reality behind the text.
